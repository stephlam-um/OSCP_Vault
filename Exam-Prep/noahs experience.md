This video demystifies the **Offensive Security Certified Professional (OCP) Plus** exam, presenting it not as an unattainable “mythical endgame boss” but as an achievable certification within approximately three months if approached strategically. The speaker, Noah, aims to share his personal experiences, insights, and study strategies to help viewers prepare effectively for the OCP exam, including advice for those cramming or seeking clarification on the exam process.

[00:36]  
Noah introduces himself as a recent **OCP Plus** exam passer, having scored an impressive **90 out of 100**. He outlines his exam experience emphasizing:

- The **passing score** threshold is **70 out of 100**.
- His exam duration spanned roughly **8 to 9 hours** to achieve the 90 score, including breaks.
- He reached the passing score (~70 points) within about **5 hours**.
- After passing, Noah took an entire day to finalize the report portion, underscoring the importance of report writing after the practical exam portion.
- His report was a **55-page professional penetration testing document** based on the **OffSec PEN 200 course template**, which he modified slightly for flow and relevance.
- Feedback from Offensive Security (OPSAC) came within **2 days**, confirming his certification.

[03:08]  
Proctoring during the OCP exam was another common question Noah addresses:

- The proctoring setup was described as **“boring” and low-key**, with **no face-to-face video engagement**, primarily text chat for support.
- Noah expected a more interactive or celebratory environment but found it **simple and non-intrusive**.
- He reassures viewers that proctoring should not be a source of anxiety, becoming routine after about two hours.
- This low-key proctoring system facilitated a smooth exam experience.

[04:51]  
Noah then shifts focus to **how to study effectively for the OCP exam**, stressing several foundational pillars:

- **Consistency** in study is paramount and should be treated like any rigorous academic exam.
- Repeated study of the **course material** from the PEN 200 course is essential since the exam tests this knowledge.
- Avoid the common mistake of skipping the PEN 200 course expecting to pass the exam without sufficient preparation.
- Recognizes the **long duration** of the PEN 200 course, highlighting that preparation is a "months-long" commitment.
- Advises scheduling **multiple 2-3 hour study blocks per week** based on individual calendar availability.
- Adjustments should be made based on personal circumstances (e.g., childcare responsibilities).
- Emphasizes showing up for scheduled study blocks, even just a few days per week, to maintain steady progress without burnout.
- Noah personally finished the PEN 200 course in **2 to 3 months** due to a time constraint but cautions against rushing the learning process for mastery.

Regarding pacing and customization approaches:

|Preparation Approach|Description|Recommended For|Risks & Notes|
|---|---|---|---|
|Speedrun Track|Skipping non-essential parts of PEN 200, diving straight into challenge labs.|Experienced professionals, those already proficient, under time constraints.|May miss deep learning, less comprehensive knowledge. Not recommended for beginners.|
|Balanced Approach|Spread out course study over weeks/months without pressure, focusing on exam-relevant sections.|Casual learners, those without strict deadlines, seeking balanced knowledge.|More time-consuming but thorough and less stressful.|

- Noah underscores that everyone’s path is unique depending on prior knowledge, schedule, and learning goals.

[08:43]  
The **most critical component** of OCP study, according to Noah, is the **challenge labs**:

- Labs provide **real hands-on experience** similar to platforms like _Hack The Box_ or _Try Hack Me_.
- They enable practical application of theoretical knowledge from PEN 200 on real or simulated networks.
- Labs help develop practical skills such as enumeration techniques, time management for exploits, and privilege escalation strategies.
- Vital for developing and refining a **personal pentesting workflow**.
- Noah revised his workflow three times during the labs, demonstrating their importance in preparing for the exam’s format and pressure.

[09:54]  
Noah lists specifics about his lab practice regimen:

|Lab Name|Coverage|Purpose/Outcome|
|---|---|---|
|Module 27|“Assembling the pieces”|Guided pen test walkthrough, strong foundational knowledge.|
|Secura Lab|Foothold machines only|Focused on getting footholds; foundational entry techniques.|
|MedTech|Full lab|Challenging, took several days, high value in exam prep.|
|Reelia|Full lab|Similar depth to MedTech, key practice experience.|
|Skylock|Foothold machines only|Addressed weakness in foothold identification/exploitation.|
|OCP A, B, C|Full labs, done week before exam|Most exam-like labs; high confidence booster.|

- Emphasis on focusing on foothold machines when weak in that area.
- Using last week to focus heavily on labs closely mimicking exam scenarios reinforced confidence.

[12:13]  
Recommendations for different study profiles:

- **Speedrun Choice:**
    
    - For those already skilled or with industry experience, skipping much of the course and focusing on labs and key modules (OCP A, B, C) may suffice.
    - Noah admitted skipping modules 2-7 (introductory content), module 21, and modules 28-31.
    - This strategy sacrifices deep learning but expedites certification.
- **Balanced Approach:**
    
    - For most learners without urgent deadlines.
    - Study steadily and thoroughly with weekly blocks over months.
    - Optional skipping of non-exam objectives like the cloud section of PEN 200, since cloud topics are **not covered in the OCP Plus exam**.
- **Community Engagement:**
    
    - Joining the **OffSec Discord** community is highly recommended.
    - Provides near-instant support, mentorship, and resources during lab work and exam prep.
    - A valuable aid in overcoming challenges and accelerating learning.
